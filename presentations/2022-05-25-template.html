Mission

If you want to build a ship, don't drum up the men to gather wood, divide the work, and give orders. Instead, teach them to yearn for the vast and endless sea.

There's nothing inherently bad about having a pithy slogan, like “organize the world's information” to describe your company's mission. The mission statement tries to make clear to anyone that might not know the purpose of the company, what that purpose is.

Product Vision

The product vision describes the future you are trying to create.

Most people I meet, when they show me their "product vision", what they are really showing me is their "mission statement". They are confusing a slogan about their purpose, with a product vision.

  • A product vision keeps us focused on the customer.
  • A product vision shows us why this work is meaningful.
    A list of features on a roadmap is not meaningful. How you can positively impact the lives of users and customers is meaningful.
  • A strong product vision serves as one of our most powerful recruiting tools for strong product people.

The best — maybe the only? — real, direct measure of "innovation" is change in human behaviour. It may not be a self driving car, for example Slack change a lot.

Product Principles

Product principles refer to the nature of the product you are building, and they help you to get a much clearer picture of what you believe in and what is important.
